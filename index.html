Health Passport Guidance

Health Passport Guidance

The Health Passport is designed for staff who have a long-term condition which hinders their performance or attendance at work. This includes non-visible and undiagnosed conditions that have a substantial and long-term impact on the employee and may be classed as a disability.

According to the Equality Act 2010, a disability is a condition that affects you physically or mentally and has a "substantial" and "long-term" negative effect on daily activities. Employers have a legal duty to offer reasonable adjustments to any employee who has a disability as defined in the Act.

  • ‚Äòsubstantial‚Äô is more than minor or trivial, e.g., it takes much longer than it usually would to complete a daily task like getting dressed.
  • ‚Äòlong-term‚Äô means 12 months or more, e.g., a breathing condition that develops because of a lung infection.
  • A progressive condition is one that gets worse over time. People with progressive conditions can be classed as disabled.
  • Automatic qualification: A person automatically meets the disability definition under the Equality Act 2010 from the day they are diagnosed with HIV infection, cancer, or multiple sclerosis.

The Health Passport is intended to help employees access the support they may need and support them to manage their health in work. They should be encouraged and supported to communicate their condition upon joining the organisation, when they change role, department, or move to another NHS employer. The passport can remain with an employee throughout their NHS career.

Where to find it

The form can be accessed on the Intranet under Reasonable Adjustments Toolkit.

How to use it

For new staff, the Health Passport can be discussed during their onboarding and induction. It is important that the passport is used positively, and the individual understands that its purpose is to support them at work. The information shared should be handled respectfully and with compassion. It can also be used as a tool to have ongoing conversations around an individual‚Äôs needs in the workplace.

When scheduling a meeting with new or existing staff to discuss workplace support you should:

  • Assure the individual that the meeting and anything included in the passport will be held confidentially.
  • Make it clear that the focus is on supporting the individual to thrive at work.
  • Make it clear that any actions you take as a manager will be recorded and timeframes agreed.
  • Ask the individual if any adjustments have been agreed with Occupational Health and if not, ask if they would like to be referred for an assessment.
  • Encourage the individual to share their thoughts throughout the meeting.
  • Ensure any actions agreed with your staff member are reasonable for the organisation, team and department.

Suggested ways to open a conversation with an employee

  • ‚ÄúI would really like to use this time today to understand more about you and how I can support you at work.‚Äù
  • ‚ÄúCan you explain what helps you thrive at work?‚Äù
  • ‚ÄúI would like to discuss the information in your Health Passport so we can look at providing the right equipment and space for you to excel in your role.‚Äù

Suggested ways to open a conversation with a line manager

  • ‚ÄúI would really like to request some time with you to discuss my Health Passport.‚Äù
  • ‚ÄúI feel I need some support in work, and these are the areas I want to discuss‚Ä¶‚Äù

If you need any further support or advice prior to meeting with your employee or manager, please contact your HR Advisor or HR Business Partner.

How to store this record

Our systems will allow documents to be held securely. It is the responsibility of the individual to look after their own personal copy of the Health Passport.

How to update it

The Passport can be used as a live document and can be revisited as the condition or needs of the individual change. Any changes should be recorded by the individual and their line manager in the space provided on the form.

Confidentiality

Any information provided by an individual is treated as sensitive data and must be kept securely. We advise that you refer to our privacy/confidentiality policy.

Please note: It‚Äôs crucial the employee gives their consent for the information to be shared wider than their immediate line manager.
